What is SB4?


SB4 is a law in Texas that allows local law enforcement to question a person's immigration status during an arrest or lawful stop. This law aims to encourage Texas law enforcement officers to cooperate with federal immigration officials.

Police are not required to ask every detainee about their immigration status, but this law can lead police officers to discriminate towards people they perceive as illegal immigrants.

The Status of SB4


Cities and counties over the state tested SB4 in government court contending that it was illegal. On August 30, 2017, the court issued a request incidentally hindering the greater part of SB4 from becoming effective. The State of Texas instantly appeal the local court's starter directive request to the 5th Circuit Court of Appeals and moved for a crisis stay order. After a hearing on September 22, 2017, in New Orleans, the 5th Circuit Court unblocked just the detainer command, leaving generally set up the directive. On November 7, 2017, the Court of Appeals held a hearing on the full merits. On March 13, 2018, the Court of Appeals issued a decision that unblocked the vast majority of the law, enabling SB4 to go into effect.
